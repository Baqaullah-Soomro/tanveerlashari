EmailJS Template Setup Guide

Step 1: Navigate to Email Templates

In your EmailJS dashboard, go to the "Email Templates" section and select your contact form template.

Step 2: Set the Recipient Email Address

In the template editor, locate the "To Email" field. This is where you specify who will receive the emails sent from your contact form.

Important: Set the "To Email" field to: tanveerlasharilibrarian@gmail.com

This ensures all contact form submissions are sent to this email address.

The "To Email" field is typically located in the email settings section of the template editor:

  • To Email: tanveerlasharilibrarian@gmail.com
  • From Name: Can be set to {{from_name}} (the sender's name)
  • Reply-To: Should be set to {{reply_to}} (the sender's email)

Step 3: Save Your Template

After setting the recipient email address, click the "Save" button to apply your changes.

Step 4: Test Your Contact Form

Return to your website and test the contact form to ensure emails are being sent to the correct address.

Why This Matters

In EmailJS, the recipient email address is configured in the template settings on the EmailJS dashboard, not in your website's code. This is a security feature that prevents unauthorized changes to the recipient address.

If your contact form is not sending emails to the correct address, it's likely because the "To Email" field in your template is not properly configured.
